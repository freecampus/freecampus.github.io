## Reason with confidence

Mathematics is not a collection of tricks to memorize. It is a way to describe structure, test ideas, and make careful arguments. This path emphasizes why methods work—not only how to use them.

<div class="module-list">
  <div><span>01</span><h3>Numbers & structure</h3><p>Build strong intuition for quantities, operations, and relationships.</p></div>
  <div><span>02</span><h3>Algebraic thinking</h3><p>Use symbols to express patterns and reason about the unknown.</p></div>
  <div><span>03</span><h3>Functions & change</h3><p>Understand how quantities depend on and vary with one another.</p></div>
  <div><span>04</span><h3>Geometry & space</h3><p>Explore shape, measurement, transformations, and proof.</p></div>
  <div><span>05</span><h3>Data & uncertainty</h3><p>Draw responsible conclusions from variation and evidence.</p></div>
</div>
