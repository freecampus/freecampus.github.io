## Learn to speak to computers

Python is a powerful first programming language because its ideas are visible and its syntax stays out of the way. This learning path begins with expressions and data, then steadily builds toward programs that solve real problems.

<div class="module-list">
  <div><span>01</span><h3>Values & expressions</h3><p>How programs represent information and produce results.</p></div>
  <div><span>02</span><h3>Variables & decisions</h3><p>Give ideas names and teach programs to choose a path.</p></div>
  <div><span>03</span><h3>Loops & collections</h3><p>Work effectively with sequences and repeated actions.</p></div>
  <div><span>04</span><h3>Functions</h3><p>Break a complex problem into clear, reusable parts.</p></div>
  <div><span>05</span><h3>Files & data</h3><p>Read, transform, and preserve information.</p></div>
  <div><span>06</span><h3>Build a project</h3><p>Bring everything together in a program of your own.</p></div>
</div>
